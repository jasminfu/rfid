Introduction

This page showcases our RFID nest box, which reads 125kHz PIT tags, monitors motion occurring inside the nest box, logs temperature data every 20 minutes, and takes a picture of the nest box contents when motion is detected.

These nest boxes are designed to monitor nests and study parental provisioning of Eastern Bluebirds, but the size can easily be adapted to study other species.

Power can be adapted to the study area and needs, as the nest box can function with a solar panel or a 12V battery with corresponding wiring adjustments

Preview


An image of our nest box deployed at Panola Mountain State Park. An RFID antenna is embedded at the entrance of the box. A PIT tagged bird will be detected every time it perches or moves through the nest box.


Power can be adapted to the study area and needs, as the nest box can function with a solar panel or a 12V battery with corresponding wiring adjustments.
Our solar panel faces the south and functions with a one-cell battery and a Wagner board.


A temperature probe logs the interior temperature of the nest box every 20 minutes.


A PIR motion sensor, a small camera and infrared light bulbs to illuminate the interior of the nest box point towards the bottom of the box. They are encased in a project box with a transparent lid, along with the microcontroller, to protect the electronic components.
The cables sticking out of the project box lead to the RFID antenna, temperature probe and power system. They are connected to the project box with aviation connectors, which are waterproof.


A closer look at the electronics of the system. The nest box relies on a Seeed Studio XIAO ESP32 S3 Sense, which comes with a camera, SD card module and WiFi functions. The green circuit board on the lower right is a WL-125, which reads 125kHz PIT tags. A real-time-clock (square-like circuit board next to the WL-125) with its own external battery is incorporated to keep time when the device is sleeping or suddenly loses power.
