Tools Required

We recommend the following for a successful build:

  • Drill for screws and creating holes on project box
  • Saw for cutting the PVC fence post

Building the feeder

Feeder Insert 3D Print

The feeder insert was designed by Brendan Morrissey from Georgia Gwinnett College. Visit our GitHub repository to download.

The design is intended to fit into a 5 inch by 5 inch PVC fence post. We recommend printing in PETG filament for UV and heat resistance, but you may experiment with other materials.

There is a slot for you to insert the factory antenna at the front of the feeder insert, and a hole in the lower corner that will allow the antenna cable to run to the back of the feeder, and out to the project box.

PVC Tube

Cut out a section of PVC tubing

Search "how to cut vinyl fence post" online for instructions. We cut our tube into 11 inch sections. You can adjust the size according to your needs.

Drill a hole into the PVC

Drill a hole of 1.5 inch diameter near the bottom of the PVC tube.

It is best to have the feeder insert on hand when determining where to drill your hole.

The PVC tube stops all of the seed from flowing out of the feeder insert. In order for this to work, the hole must be placed slightly above the bottom edge of the feeder insert's opening.

Fit the insert into the PVC tube

Place the insert into the PVC tube to check the fit. Place seed into your PVC tube to test whether the seed flows out of the tube.

Deployment

Getting feeder visits

We recommend the following actions:

  1. Place an easily recognizable feeder at the location where you want birds to visit.
  2. Once birds are reliably visiting the feeder location that you set up, swap out your previously recognizable feeder with the PVC tube feeder.
  3. Place a bit of suet at the feeder entrance to train birds to recognize the PVC tube as a food source.
