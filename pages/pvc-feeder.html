Introduction

Below outlines the steps to build a bird feeder with square PVC fence post capable of housing the factory antenna of a WL-134 or WL-125 RFID module.

Tools Required

We recommend the following for a successful build:

  • Drill for screws and creating holes
  • Step drill bit for creating a hole for the aviation connector on the project box
  • #2 Phillips drill insert bit or screwdriver for screwing the project box onto the dog ears.
  • Wood drill bits (various small sizes according to your hardware) for creating holes on the PVC and wooden lid.
  • Saw for cutting the PVC fence post. There are many options for cutting PVC fence post.
  • Hot Glue Gun (recommended) for securing the antenna to the feeder insert.

Building the feeder

Please consult the video for more detailed explanation of each step. Each video thumbnail below looks the same but links to the timestamps of the corresponding header.

3D Print

This design functions with a 3D printed insert.

The feeder insert was designed by Brendan Morrissey from Georgia Gwinnett College. Visit our GitHub repository to download.

The design is intended to fit into a 5 inch by 5 inch PVC fence post. We recommend printing in PETG filament for UV and heat resistance, but you may experiment with other materials.

There is a slot for you to insert the factory antenna at the front of the feeder insert, and a hole in the lower corner that will allow the antenna cable to run to the back of the feeder, and out to the project box.

If you do not own a 3D printer, you may also send this design to a 3D printing service to get it printed.

PVC Tube

Cut out a section of PVC tubing

There are many methods for cutting vinyl fence posts. We recommend searching online for instructions that fit your capabilities. We cut our tube into 11 inch sections. You can size it according to your needs.

Drill a hole into the PVC

Drill a hole of 1.5 inch diameter near the bottom of the PVC tube.

It is best to have the feeder insert on hand when determining where to drill your hole.

The PVC tube stops all of the seed from flowing out of the feeder insert. In order for this to work, the hole must be placed slightly above the bottom edge of the feeder insert's opening. On the other hand, do not drill the hole too high, as the feeder opening can get too small for seed to dispense or for birds to feed.

Create a divot for the antenna cable to pass through (optional)

Use a file or a drill to create a half-circle divot on the lower left corner of the side opposite the feeder opening (where the antenna wire will exit the feeder insert).

Creating the lid

Below details the instructions for securing a wooden lid onto the PVC tube. If you are using other means to create the lid (e.g. a PVC end cap), you may skip these steps.

Attach the wooden lid

  1. Place the 5 x 5 inch wooden board on top of the PVC tube feeder. Place the piano hinge with the spine facing outward, positioned at the gap between the wooden board and the PVC tube.
  2. Mark the holes where #4-40 3/8-inch screws (for the PVC side) and the sharp point screws (for the wood side) will be located. Drill small holes at the marks on the PVC side. Drill shallow holes on the marks of the wooden side.
  3. Place #4-40 3/8-inch screws on the PVC side of the piano hinge. Place a washer onto each screw. Screw in the nuts.
  4. With a Phillips drill bit, attach the piano hinge on the wooden side with sharp point screws.

Attach the latch

  1. Place the clasp on the front of the feeder and center of the wooden board. Attach it to the board with sharp point screws.
  2. Place the buckle onto the clasp and mark the bottom of the where the buckle will sit on the PVC.
  3. Mark the location and drill the hole for the bottom hole of the buckle (where the screw will attach). Insert a #4-40 3/8-inch screw. Place a washer and nut on the screw, on the inside of the PVC tube, to secure this screw.
  4. Mark the location and drill the hole for the top hole of the buckle (where the other nylon bolt will attach). Again, insert a #4-40 3/8-inch screw into this hole. Use a washer and nut to secure this screw.

Attaching the Feeder Insert

Bolt the square mounting plate onto the feeder insert

  1. Position the mounting plate such that approximately 0.75 inches sticks out from the back of the feeder insert. This will act as a lip for the project box.
  2. Drill 4 small holes on the feeder insert, with the holes positioned at the innermost corners of the slits on the mounting plate.
  3. Insert 4 #8-32 1-inch screws to fix the mounting plate onto the feeder insert.
  4. Attach nuts for each screw. Removing the feeder insert from the PVC tube will make this step easier.

Glue the antenna to the feeder insert

Place the antenna into the square slot in the feeder insert, and use hot glue to secure both together. Secure the wire to the insert as well.

Place the insert into the PVC tube to check the fit. Place seed into your PVC tube to test how the seed flows out of the tube.

Screw the PVC tube to the feeder insert

  1. Drill a small hole on the side of the feeder where the antenna is not located.
  2. Place a screw (any size) into the hole and screw tight to fix the tube to the feeder insert.

Project Box

Attach tabs onto project box and PVC tube

  1. Screw in 2 tabs onto the top corners of the project box. The screws are included with the project box.
  2. Turn tabs inwards and position the project box onto the PVC tube. The bottom of the box should be fully flush with the lip of the metal mounting plate for the pole. The holes on the tab should be located far enough away from the curved corners of the square PVC to make room for the nut inside the PVC tube (screwed on later).
  3. Mark holes where the tabs will attach to the PVC tube. Drill and insert #8-32 1-inch screws in each hole.
  4. Attach a washer and nut onto each screw on the inside of the PVC tube.

Affix an L bracket into the project box wall (optional)

We chose to put our antenna cable on the bottom of the project box for waterproofing purposes. If you place your cable elsewhere on the project box, you can skip the steps involving the L bracket and the mounting plate, as you do not need to mount the battery onto the top of the project box.

  1. Place your 12V battery into the project box and mark the bottom of the battery. You will adhere the L bracket at this location.
  2. Stick velcro strips onto the L bracket and the marked location in the project box.
  3. Affix the L bracket onto the project box wall with the velcro strips.
  4. Place battery into the box to check fit.

Secure electronic PCB boards onto a mounting plate (optional)

You may purchase a mounting plate and cut it in half with a hand saw or other means. You may also use a piece of thin wood, and drill your own holes to fit the spacing of your PCB board.

  1. Position nylon hex spacers onto mounting plate according to the spacing of your PCB board.
  2. Screw hex nuts onto the backside of the mounting plate to fix the spacers onto the board (not shown in the video). Attach the screws on the frontside of the mounting plate to attach the PCB board onto the mounting plate.
  3. Screw the mounting plate onto the project box (an adequately sized screw may come with your purchase of a mounting plate).

Attach the aviation connector(s) onto the project box

  1. Use a step drill bit to drill a hole onto the project box. The hole should be big enough to fit the aviation connector with the JST connector attached.
  2. Put the dust plug onto the aviation connector without the JST connector. On the side with the JST connector, put on the split washer and hex nut. Hand-screw the nut onto the inside of the project box.
  3. Plug in the JST connector to the RFID module (or other peripherals if needed).
  4. Plug in the aviation connector from the antenna cable to the project box's aviation connector.

Deployment

Staking the feeder into the ground

We recommend tilting the pole slightly forward to accommodate the center of gravity of the feeder.

Getting feeder visits

We recommend the following actions:

  1. Place an easily recognizable feeder at the location where you want birds to visit.
  2. Once birds are reliably visiting the feeder location that you set up, swap out your previously recognizable feeder with the PVC tube feeder.
  3. Place a bit of suet at the feeder entrance to train birds to recognize the PVC tube as a food source.
